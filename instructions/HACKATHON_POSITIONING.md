# Hackathon Positioning

## Track Strategy

The hackathon track is focused on the **best use of Agnes AI API**.

FamLens should therefore demonstrate Agnes AI as a full multimodal accessibility agent, not a single chatbot call.

## Core Positioning

The differentiator is not:

> "We built an app that describes images."

The differentiator is:

> "We built an Agnes-powered accessibility agent that helps visually impaired elderly users participate in family photo conversations."

## Why This Matters

Family communication is increasingly visual. Photos sent through WhatsApp carry emotional context, family updates, celebrations, travel moments, and everyday memories.

A generic image description may say:

> "There are three people near a cake."

FamLens should say:

> "This looks like a birthday celebration. A child is smiling beside a cake that appears to say Happy Birthday Ethan. The mood feels warm and happy."

## Best Use of Agnes API

FamLens should show Agnes AI being used across multiple stages:

```text
Image understanding
  -> OCR text reading
  -> Family context matching
  -> Elder-friendly summarisation
  -> Emotional explanation
  -> Voice Q&A
  -> Reply generation
```

This makes Agnes the intelligence layer of the product.

## Demo Message

> FamLens uses Agnes AI not as a chatbot, but as a multimodal accessibility agent. It sees, reads, explains, answers, and helps the user reply.

## What Judges Should Notice

- The user problem is specific and human.
- The AI is essential to the workflow.
- The app uses multimodal understanding.
- The app demonstrates practical accessibility value.
- The app handles uncertainty responsibly.
- The app helps the user participate, not just consume information.
- The demo is emotionally memorable.

## What Not To Claim

Do not claim:

- "Apple and Samsung cannot describe images."
- "We can perfectly identify family members."
- "The AI always knows who is in the photo."
- "This replaces human caregivers."
- "This is a medical accessibility solution."

Better claim:

> Existing accessibility tools provide useful generic descriptions. FamLens focuses on personal, elder-friendly, family-context-aware photo understanding and reply support.

## Winning Demo Arc

1. Show the family photo problem.
2. Show a generic caption that feels emotionally flat.
3. Show FamLens producing a warm, spoken, personal summary.
4. Ask a follow-up question.
5. Read text in the image.
6. Generate a reply.
7. Show privacy and uncertainty controls.
8. End with emotional inclusion: the user can now participate in the family moment.
